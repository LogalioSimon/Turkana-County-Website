About Turkana County

Turkana County is a county in the former Rift Valley Province of Kenya. Its home to the Turkana people. It is Kenya's largest county by land area of 77,597.8 km² followed by Marsabit County with an area of 66,923.1 km². It is bordered by the countries of Uganda to the west; South Sudan and Ethiopia, including the disputed Ilemi Triangle, to the north and northeast; and Lake Turkana to the east. To the south and east, neighbouring counties in Kenya are West Pokot, Baringo and Samburu Counties, while Marsabit County is on the opposite (i.e. eastern) shore of Lake Turkana. Turkana's capital and largest town is Lodwar. The county had a population of 926,976 at the 2019 census, projected (2024) 1.048M.

Culture

Discover the rich cultural heritage of the Turkana people, celebrated through traditional dances, rituals, and ceremonies.

Celebrating Turkana's Traditions

The Turkana people have a rich cultural heritage with ceremonies that signify important events in their community. These celebrations, which include initiation rites, marriage rituals, and dance performances, are filled with meaning and are passed down through generations.

Did You Know?

Turkana beadwork is not just decorative; it holds symbolic meaning representing age, status, and achievements in the community. Each design tells a unique story of the individual wearing it.

Landmarks of Turkana

Lake Turkana

The largest desert lake in the world, also known as the Jade Sea.

Turkana Boy Site

The site of the most complete early human skeleton discovery.

Hike at the hills in Lodwar

Get to climb one of the many hills and enjoy an uninterrupted view of Lodwar town and its environs.

Namoratunga Standing Stones

Dancing Stones of Namoratunga are undoubtedly one of East Africa’s most intriguing archaeological sites..

Turkana Economy

Turkana County boasts a diverse economy driven by natural resources, livestock, and traditional crafts. Below are some of the key sectors:

Livestock Farming

Pastoralism forms the backbone of Turkana's economy, with cattle, goats, and camels playing a central role.

Fishing Industry

Lake Turkana supports a vibrant fishing community, providing income and food for thousands.

Oil and Gas

The Lokichar Basin oil reserves represent a significant opportunity for economic growth.

Tourism

Natural wonders and cultural heritage attract visitors, boosting local revenue.
